Regions:

  • Chiapas: Known for its lush landscapes and vibrant indigenous cultures, Chiapas produces some of Mexico's finest coffee beans. Grown in the southernmost part of the country, Chiapas coffee often exhibits a medium body with hints of chocolate and fruity acidity.
  • Veracruz: Nestled along the Gulf of Mexico, Veracruz is another key coffee-growing region in Mexico. Its coffees are prized for their bright acidity, balanced by a smooth, creamy texture. Expect flavors ranging from citrus to floral, making Veracruz coffee a delightful choice for enthusiasts seeking complexity.
  • Oaxaca: With its mountainous terrain and rich biodiversity, Oaxaca is celebrated for its organic coffee production. Oaxacan beans are renowned for their full-bodied richness, often accompanied by notes of caramel and spice. This region's commitment to sustainable farming practices adds an extra layer of appeal for conscientious consumers.
